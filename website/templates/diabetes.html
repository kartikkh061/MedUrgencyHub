~~~Diabetes: Epidemic of the Century~~~



One of the most challenging problem which is gripping the world into its hands is the problem of very high glucose levels in the human body which is commonly known as Diabetes.
In clinical terms, Diabetes mellitus(DM) can be, commonly defined as a group of metabolic disorders characterized by a high blood level sugar over a prolonged period of time. Symptoms often include frequent urination, increased thirst, and increased appetite. This is not a single hereditary disease but a heterogeneous group of diseases, -all of which ultimately lead to an elevation of glucose in the blood (hyperglycemia) and loss of glucose in the urine as hyperglycemia increases.
Usually 2 hormones, namely, Glucagon>(increases blood glucose level) and Insulin (decreases blood glucose level), together help in maintaining a healthy glucose level in the body. Both of these hormones are produced by the pancreas which also plays a major role in the digestion of food. Any defect in the organ or its hormone producing cells can lead to Diabetes.


Types of Diabetes:



Major studies classify this metabolic Disorder in 3 types:



Now let us learn about some of the most common disorders related to the digestive system:

  • Diabetes Mellitus Type-1: it is insulin -dependent diabetes Mellitus and is also known as Juvenile Onset Diabetes. It most commonly develops in people younger than 20 years. It's auto-immune disorder in which the body's immune system destroys the cells responsible for the production of insulin.
  • Diabetes Mellitus Type-2: it is Non- Insulin dependent Diabetes Mellitus and is also known as Maturity Onset Diabetes because it occurs later in life. Its arises not from the shortage of insulin but because target cells became less sensitive to insulin. Obesity can be a cause of this type of diabetes.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Gestational diabetes occurs when there is a high blood glucose level during pregnancy. As pregnancy progresses, the developing baby has a greater need for glucose. Hormone changes during pregnancy also affect the action of insulin, which brings about high blood glucose levels.
  • Some of the signs and symptoms of diabetes are:
    • Increased thirst
    • Frequent urination
    • Extreme Hunger
    • Fatigue
    • Unexplained weight loss
    • Irritability


    Ways to prevent and tackle diabetes:


    • Exercise Regularly: Regular exercise can help you lose weight and increase insulin sensitivity. Increased insulin sensitivity means your cells are better able to use the available in sugar in your bloodstream.
    • Drink Water and Stay Hydrated: Drinking enough water may help you keep your blood sugar levels within healthy limits.
    • Eat real food rather than processed food: Real food (fruits, vegetables, fish, meat, seeds, and nuts) will help you avoid refined carbohydrates, added sugars and trans fats, all of which promote unhealthy weight gain and increase the risk of diabetes.
    • Use of Natural herbs with anti-diabetic properties like:
      • Aloe vera
      • Bilberry extract
      • Bitter melon
      • Fenugreek
      • Ginger
      • Okra
    • Say No to Alcohol: alcohol consumption is directly related to diabetes. It damages the pancreas and itself has a lot of carbohydrates.
