~~~Your Gut Feeling: A Healthier Digestive System~~~


It is commonly said that, the to a Man's way heart goes through his stomach."
But the actual fact is that this is applicable to every individual in every age of the life. Having a healthy digestive system improves the overall health of the body as it plays a crucial role in absorbing the essential nutrients from the food we eat. The food that we eat is broken down to simple substances in our digestive system.

This process of conversion of food into simple absorbable forms is called Digestion. Now let us learn a bit more about Our digestive system.

How does the Digestive system works?


Now let us learn about some of the most common disorders related to the digestive system:

  • Jaundice: the bile pigments of the liver increases in the blood causing Yellowness in the skin and the Eyes. The liver is also affected.
  • Vomiting: it's the ejection of stomach contents through the mouth. This reflex action is controlled by the Vomit center in the lower back part of the brain. Feeling of Nausea precedes vomiting.
  • Diarrhea: the abnormal frequency of bowel movement and increased liquidity of fecal discharge is known as diarrhea. It reduces absorption of food.
  • Constipation: in this disorder, the faeces is retained within the rectum as the bowel movement occurs irregularly.
  • Indigestion: the food is not properly digested leading to a feeling of fullness. The causes are inadequate enzyme secretion, anxiety, food poisoning over eating and spicy food. Gaining knowledge about the working of the system and the related disorders, increases the curiosity to know about the improve ways to its health as well.

So let us learn about some common and natural to ways raise our digestive health.


Ways to Improve Upon Digestion and Digestive System:


  • Taking food rich in high Fiber: Consuming a high-fiber diet that's rich in whole grains, vegetables, fruits , and legumes can improve your digestive health. A high-fiber diet helps to keep food moving through your digestive tract, making you less likely to get constipated.
  • Chewing the food properly: The process of digestion starts right in the mouth itself. Chewing food results in producing saliva in the mouth which in turn releases digestive enzymes which help in digestion. More we chew, more saliva is released and it can help tackle problems like indigestion and heart-burns.
  • Staying Always Hydrated: Water plays a vital role in digestion. It helps in preventing constipation and other digestion related issues. We must take at least 1.5-2 liters of water on a daily basis.
  • Doing Exercise Regularly: Exercise can have a very positive impact on our digestive system. It can help relieve stress, strengthen abdominal muscles, and stimulate food to move through the large intestine as well.
  • Cut back on Alcohol drinking: many people experience diarrhea after alcohol drinking. This is because the digestive system gets inflamed and reduces water absorption which leads to such a problem.
  • Stop smoking: smoking can lead to a lot of digestive disorders like peptic ulcers, heartburn and worsen conditions involving Crohn's disease. One major advantage of quitting smoking that that it reverses some of the ill effects that occurred to you smoking quickly.
  • Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables: Fruits and vegetables are among the most important foods for supplying the vitamins, minerals and fiber our body needs for good health and normal immune function.
  • Taking fermented loads: Fermented foods such as probiotic yogurt, may help improve digestive problems. Fermented foods have been partially or wholly broken down by microorganisms such as bacteria. These microorganisms work to preserve food, and they may also benefit gut health. Some fermented foods include:
    • Probiotic yogurt
    • Sourdough bread
    • Sauerkraut
    • Kefir
    • Miso
