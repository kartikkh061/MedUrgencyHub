~~~Healthy Teeth, Healthy Smile~~~


It's said that we should always keep smiling, as it makes the world, a beautiful place to Live in.

Our teeth form a major part of our smile and plays very important roles in our daily life. One being, playing the key role in the digestion of the food we eat, starting in the Oral cavity itself.

Let's learn a bit more about teeth!


Majority of mammals including humans, have 2 set of teeth during their life, first is the temporary set also called milk or deciduous teeth and second being the permanent set. This type of dentition is called DIPHYODONT. An adult human has 32 permanent teeth, which are of 4 different types, namely INCISORS, CANINES, PREMOLARS and MOLARS. It's called Heterodont dentition. Out of all the types, only Pre-Molars are the teeth which appears only once in lifetime.

The hard-chewing surface covering teeth is made up of Enamel. It's the hardest substance of human body, secreted by Ameloblast cells. Each tooth consists of 3 parts, namely Crown, Neck and Root. The Crown remains capped by enamel. Both crown and root are composed of dentine, produced by Odontoblast cells, inside which a central pulp cavity is present in which there is blood and nerve supply.

Having so much knowledge about such a small teeth rises the curiosity to learn about the problems and remedies related to keep our teeth healthy and safe.


Common problems related to our teeth:


  • Cavities: Cavities are also called tooth decay and are common. These are areas of the tooth that have been permanently damaged and may even have holes in them. They occur when bacteria, food, and acid coats your teeth and form a plaque.
  • Periodontitis: The infection can spread to your jaw and bones. It can also cause an inflammatory response throughout the body.
  • Oral cancer: Use of tobacco is the major risk factor leading to oral cancer. Oral cancers include cancer of the:
    • Gums
    • Tongue
    • Lips
    • Cheek
    • floor of the mouth i.e. hard and soft palate
  • Gum disease: Gingivitis is a gum disease that is reversible but, if not treated accordingly. may lead to a more serious problem called periodontal disease.
  • Root Infection: A root infection occurs when bacteria infects the root part of your teeth. It enters the center of your tooth tooth and attacks the pulp tissue inside. Eventually. an abscess will form indicating that the root infection has developed to a more severe case of ulcers or sores.
  • Bleeding or swollen gums after brushing.
  • Chronic bad breath.
  • Sudden sensitivity to hot and cold temperatures or beverages.
  • Pain or toothache
  • Cracked or broken teeth
  • Frequent dry mouth
